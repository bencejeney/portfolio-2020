Videography

YouTube as a hobby

I played a lot of Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas back in 2012, it is still my favourite game of all time. I have met some people online who made drift videos from that game, I immediately got interested in making similar videos. I started off my YouTube channel in 2013, with pretty much amateur content and zero knowledge in video editing, using a cracked Sony Vegas Pro 11. By the time, I have learnt several techniques of editing, including unique effects, transitions, I made some bigger projects with friends, collaborations, I got noticed by lots of people on YouTube, even a Canadian YouTuber with almost 2 million subscribers shared my videos several times. I felt so good being part of a community, and even better making the best content for the community.

I even had a drift team in GTA SA:MP, which I declare the most successful team in making flashy and exciting team collaborations.

Unfortunately, the community became more and more inactive, so I decided to move on and make videos from other games, like Counter-Strike: Global Offensive, Grand Theft Auto V and Forza Horizon and also from the real life.

Later, I applied to video jobs which I found on the internet, and I also helped my friend, KD Drawings promoting his artwork.

Bullseye Drift Krew Intro

Description

This intro animation was made for a GTA San Andreas Multiplayer drift team.

The team logo rendered in 3D fades in, then the screen starts flashing and glitching. The sound effect is getting louder, until the logo "explodes". Once it explodes, a huge fire appears in the background, and the logo changes to 2D, its edges are burning. After a while, the logo stops burning, the flag waves constantly, until the screen fades out.

Programs I've used for making this intro:
Adobe After Effects CS6
Sony Vegas Pro 13.0
