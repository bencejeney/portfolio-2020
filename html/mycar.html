My car: Škoda Felicia

Type: Hatchback
Colour: Nautical Blue

Fuel: Petrol
Drivetrain: Front Wheel Drive
Gearbox: 5-speed manual
Top speed: 180 km/h
Extras: BRAID Winrace TA 6x14" alloy rims, Mac Audio 800W sound system, Kenwood KDC-X5100BT head unit


Description

The Škoda Felicia, (Typ 791) was a supermini car produced by the Czech carmaker Škoda Auto from 1994 to 2001. It was the last model on Škoda's own platform, but was one of the first models to benefit from Škoda Auto's takeover by the German company Volkswagen Group. The Felicia was a reworked version of the Favorit, but had a fresh, more modern appearance, with a redesigned front end and a wider range of engines.

It was premiered in October 1994, on the Charles Bridge in Prague. Serial production of the hatchback began in October 1994, the combi in June 1995, the pick up in August 1995, and it ended in June 2001. The name Felicia was not used for the first time, resurrecting a nameplate originally used by Škoda in the 1960s for a range of two seater sports cars.

Story

How I got the car

My parents needed a new, small and affordable family car, so they chose this blue Škoda Felicia, they bought it for 300 000.- SKK, back in 1998 (which is around 10 000 € today).

I got the car for my 18th birthday in 2017, with 92 000 km on the clock, and a brand new Kenwood/MAC Audio sound system. I didn’t have a job back then, but I spent all my saved money on the car, mostly on fuel.



Things can go wrong sometimes...

I had a huge accident in December 2017, I went fast into an icy corner, the car started sliding, I lost control and went straight into the ditch, the car flipped over at least 3 times, that’s what the oncoming driver said when he stopped to help me... could’ve been worse, I wasn’t injured.

My life was like a nightmare without a car, luckily after 4 months the car was fixed and I was able to drive it again, although it wasn’t perfectly restored to its original state, and... it still isn’t. Some things have been stolen from my car as well, like the amplifier and some small toy cars which I had glued on the dashboard.



The present

Since I have a job, I spend my money on food, parties, or on my car. I want to make a perfect car out of it, which looks fantastic and is definitely fun to drive. I started off with fixing the clutch, engine problems, headlights, then in September, I was able to buy a really good-looking set of rally rims, BRAID Winrace TA. It took more than 2 months to deliver the rims, but it was worth the waiting. The car’s cornering has improved a lot since it doesn’t drive on steelies.



Plans

My plans are fixing the rust issues, repaint (or just polish) the whole chassis, replacing the broken front and rear bumpers, fog lights, buying new sport coilovers, better brakes (from a Mitsubishi Lancer Evo maybe), all this until the MOT in January.
